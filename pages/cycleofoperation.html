Open Pit Mining:

Cycle of Operations

1. RIPPING AND DOZING

"Ripping" and "Dozing" are earthmoving techniques used to prepare the mining site and facilitate subsequent activities. These processes are typically part of the pre-drilling activities.

The purpose of ripping in mining is to facilitate the removal of overburden and expose ore or mineral deposits by breaking up hard materials. On the other hand, dozing is employed to push and move materials, creating a smooth and manageable surface for drilling, blasting, and subsequent mining operations.

Equipment: bulldozer, wheel dozers, and motor graders are the most common equipment used.

2. DRILLING

Drilling creates boreholes in the rock, allowing for the precise placement of explosives. This precision is crucial for optimizing fragmentation and controlling the size of the blasted material, which enhances ore recovery and minimizes environmental impact. Additionally, drilling enables the extraction of geological information about the rock layers, aiding in mine planning and design.

Equipment: auger (weak rock), roller-bit rotary (average rock), percussion (hard rock).

3. BLASTING

The ore deposit can be mined by means of blasting in order to fracture the rock into a loadable size. Blasting parameters should be matched with mechanical machines for drilling of blast holes and charging of explosives.

Blasting method: Ammonium Nitrate-fuel Oil (ANFO) or slurry, loaded by bulk explosive trucks or by hand, firing by electrical caps or detonating cord.

5. HAULING

Hauling refers to the transport of fragmented ore or waste material from the mining face to a designated processing or stockyard. Once the ore is blasted and broken into smaller pieces, heavy-duty haul trucks are employed to move the material efficiently.

Equipment: truck, belt conveyor, dozer, scraper (soil)
