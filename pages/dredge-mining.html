Advantages

1. Most productive of all mining methods (250 to 400 yd3, or 190 to 300 m3, of gravel per employee-shift).

2. Lowest mining cost (relative cost < 5%).

3. High production rate (maximum of 9 million yd3 or 7 million m3 a year)

4. Low labor requirements (crew of 2 to 30 people).

5. Good recovery (up to 90%).

6. Continuous operation with no breakage required.

Disadvantages

1. Environmental damage can be severe; environmental protection must be exercised; outlawed in some states.

2. Moderate water requirements (600 to 800 gal/yd3, or 3000 to 4000 1/m3, of material mined).

3. Limited to unconsolidated deposits that disintegrate under hydraulic or mechanical attack.

4. High capital investment for large dredges.

5. Inflexible and unselective; limited to placer-type deposits

Types of Dredging

1. Plain-Suction: This common dredge relies on suction to remove loose debris without a cutting tool for penetrating the water body bottom.

2. Cutter-Suction: Equipped with a cutting tool, this dredge loosens material from the bottom, making it effective for hard surfaces that hinder standard suction methods.

3. Auger-Suction: Operating like an auger, this dredge bores into the bed to loosen and suction debris, making it ideal for heavy-duty sediment removal in applications like wastewater treatment.

4. Jet-Lift: This advanced equipment uses a high-volume water stream to draw in water, silt, and debris, offering efficient sediment removal.

Dredge mining involves the use of a floating vessel to mechanically or hydraulically extract minerals from placer deposits. This underwater excavation technique is typically performed from a floating vessel known as a dredge, which may include facilities for processing and waste disposal. It is a common method employed to retrieve mineral deposits from underwater locations.

Dredging methods are often subdivided into:

a. Shallow-water dredging

This method is often classified by the method of excavation and materials transport.

b. Deep-sea or marine mining method

Deep-sea mining refers to the extraction of mineral resources from the seabed, typically found in the ocean floor. This process involves deploying specialized equipment and technologies to collect valuable metals and minerals such as polymetallic nodules, polymetallic sulphides, and ferromanganese crusts from the deep ocean.

Two types of Dredging:

1. Mechanical Dredge- The placer deposits are mechanically excavated and transported.

Example: Bucket-line Dredges exhibit continuous excavation efficiency, mining, processing, and discarding tailings seamlessly. The system lacks storage options, relying on gravity to move the stream. Buckets on a ladder dig the mine face, transporting materials to a hopper that feeds the washing plant, showcasing high excavation capabilities.

2. Hydraulic Dredge (Suction Dredge)- This method, used waster as a transportation medium.

Example: Suction Cutter dredges which is employ rotating cutting arms within a basket around a suction intake. These arms break up bank material, turning it into a slurry for easy suction into the dredge. This method has proven effective in retrieving loose beach sand and offshore placer deposits.

Suction dredges, also known as hydraulic dredges which rely on the transportation of a slurry containing placer material from the excavation site to the dredge and ultimately to the designated destination. Typically, no processing occurs on the dredge itself; instead, processing is usually carried out on land.

(3) Cutter-wheel Suction Dredges

Cutter-wheel suction dredges are specialized vessels designed for the extraction of materials from underwater environments, particularly in bodies of water like rivers, lakes, or coastal areas.

These dredgers come with a rotating cutter wheel located at the suction inlet, enabling them to cut and loosen sediment or other substances on the ocean floor. The material that is loosened is subsequently drawn up through the dredge pipe, streamlining the process of excavation and transportation to the surface. Cutter-wheel suction dredgers are frequently used in diverse dredging activities such as harbor maintenance, environmental remediation, and the retrieval of minerals from underwater deposits.
