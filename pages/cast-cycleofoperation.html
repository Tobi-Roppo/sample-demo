1. Clearing and grubbing

Clearing refers to the methodical removal of vegetation, waste, and other surface barriers from the chosen mining area. This initial stage ensures a clean and accessible workstation, making following mining operations easier and reducing environmental effect. Grubbing entails the removal of roots, stumps, and other subsurface obstructions, which further prepares the site for excavation.

2. Stripping (Bench Development)

Bench formation or development entails the methodical removal of overburden or waste material in order to expose and access the mineral reserves beneath.

3. Face Sampling

Face sampling is employed to obtain representative geological and geotechnical data from the exposed coal or mineral seam. In nickel mines, face samplers take samples from crest to toe from a standard 3m bench height. Samples are taken every after 3m-5m distance.

4. Ore Extraction

Once the bench has identified grade, ore extraction happens after. The materials are systematically removed through the use of heavy machinery, such as excavators and bulldozers, to expose and extract the underlying ore bodies.

5. Loading

Excavated materials were loaded using shovels, front end loaders or hydraulic shovels.

6. Hauling

Hauling refers to the transport of fragmented ore or waste material via dump trucks from the mining face to a designated processing or stockyard.

7. Stockpiling

It is an essential process for material handling and production optimization. The extracted material is transported utilizing heavy-duty equipment such as haul trucks and conveyors to designated areas adjacent to the mining site.

8. Barging

The barging of ore materials into Landing Craft Transports (LCT) is executed in order for the ore materials to be delivered in the vessel. Specialized heavy equipment, such as front-end loaders or excavators, is employed to load the extracted ore onto conveyor systems or trucks, facilitating seamless transfer to the designated LCT.

9. Ore Shipment

Ore shipment refers to the organized transport and delivery of extracted mineral deposits from the mining site to a designated vessel. It involves checking of compliances with industry standards for quality control, documentation, and security measures to guarantee the secure shipment of the ore material.
