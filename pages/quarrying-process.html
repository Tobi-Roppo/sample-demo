The Quarrying Process

Quarrying is the process of removing rock, sand, gravel or other minerals from the ground in order to use them to produce materials for construction or other uses. So, a quarry is any such working on the surface of the earth where minerals are extracted.

Dimension stone quarrying involves extracting rectangular rock blocks with approximate sizing and shaping. Surface quarries resemble open pits, but their benches (referred to as faces) are shallower and often vertical. Quarry highwalls can be impressively tall and steep, some reaching nearly 1,000 feet (about 300 meters). While the term "quarry" is sometimes used broadly for any surface mine extracting stone, here it specifically refers to the method used to obtain intact dimension stone blocks. In contrast, crushed limestone is obtained through open-pit mining. In summary, quarrying is the mining method used to extract dimension stone from a quarry.

Open Pit Mining:

Characteristics

Advantages

1. Low capital cost; mechanization not extensive.

2. Suited to some small deposits.

3. Easily accessible; hoisting may complicate moving stone, supplies, and workers.

4. Stable walls and benches; generally, no bank support required.

5. High selectivity; can disregard or discard low-quality stone.

6. Good safety; little chance of slope failure.

Disadvantages

1. Somewhat limited by depth; usually less than 300 ft (90 m); can be up to 1,000 ft (300 m).

2. Low productivity; high labor cost.

3. Highest mining cost because of low productivity (relative cost = 100%).

4. Low production rate.

5. Inflexible; cannot easily change the mining plan at depth.

6. Waste can be 60% to 90% .

APPLICATION:

Application of Quarrying

Quarrying for dimension stone is limited to the production of specific geologic materials that have either structural or aesthetic architectural properties.

• Granite in Vermont, Wisconsin

• Bedford limestone in Indiana

• Marble in Romblon, Philippines

• Limestone quarry in Agusan del Norte
